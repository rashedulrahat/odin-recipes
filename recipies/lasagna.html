How To Make Lasagna

Description:

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering wide, flat pasta sheets with rich fillings such as a meat sauce (ragù), béchamel or ricotta sauce, and various cheeses (mozzarella and Parmesan), which is then baked

This classic lasagna recipe is made with an easy meat sauce as the base. Layer the sauce with noodles and cheese, then bake until bubbly!

Key Ingredients:

While variations exist, a classic lasagna typically includes:

  • Lasagna noodles: Traditional boiled or oven-ready sheets.
  • Meat sauce (Ragù): A hearty sauce made with ground beef and/or Italian sausage, onions, garlic, and tomatoes (crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, tomato paste).
  • Cheese mixture: A blend of ricotta (or sometimes cottage cheese), beaten egg (as a binder), Parmesan, and parsley.
  • Béchamel (white sauce): A traditional creamy white sauce made from butter, flour, and milk, often used in authentic Italian recipes.
  • Mozzarella cheese: Shredded or sliced for the bubbling, golden-brown topping and layers.

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Sauces: First, simmer the meat sauce for at least 30 minutes (longer for a richer flavor) to allow the ingredients to meld. If using, prepare the béchamel sauce separately by making a roux and gradually whisking in the milk until thickened.
  2. Cook Noodles: Boil traditional lasagna noodles to al dente according to package directions. Drain them and lay them flat on parchment paper or kitchen towels to prevent sticking.
  3. Mix the Cheese Filling: In a medium bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, beaten egg, some of the Parmesan cheese, and any desired herbs or seasonings (like parsley, salt, and pepper).
  4. Assemble the Lasagna:
    1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish to prevent sticking.
    3. Add a layer of lasagna noodles, slightly overlapping if needed.
    4. Spread a layer of the ricotta mixture over the noodles.
    5. Spoon a layer of meat sauce on top of the ricotta.
    6. Sprinkle with mozzarella and Parmesan cheeses.
    7. Repeat the layers (noodles, ricotta, meat sauce, cheeses) until all ingredients are used, ending with noodles topped with sauce and a generous amount of mozzarella and Parmesan.
  5. Bake: Cover the dish with aluminum foil (sprayed with nonstick spray to prevent sticking) and bake for 25-45 minutes. Uncover and bake for another 10-25 minutes, until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.
  6. Rest and Serve: Let the lasagna rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing to help the layers set.
